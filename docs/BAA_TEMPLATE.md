# Business Associate Agreement (BAA) Template

> **Purpose:** HIPAA requires a BAA with any vendor that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on your behalf. Use this as a starting point; have legal review before use.

---

## Parties

- **Covered Entity:** [Your organization name – e.g. Hebrew Academy for Special Children]
- **Business Associate:** [Vendor/service provider name]

---

## Recitals

- The Covered Entity discloses PHI to the Business Associate for the following purposes: [e.g. cloud hosting, database services, backup storage].
- The parties agree to comply with HIPAA and the HIPAA Privacy, Security, and Breach Notification Rules.

---

## Obligations of the Business Associate

1. **Use and disclosure:** Use and disclose PHI only as permitted by the BAA or as required by law.
2. **Safeguards:** Implement appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect PHI.
3. **Breach notification:** Report any breach of unsecured PHI to the Covered Entity without unreasonable delay (and in any event as required by HIPAA).
4. **Subcontractors:** Ensure any subcontractor that handles PHI agrees to the same restrictions and safeguards.
5. **Access and amendment:** Make PHI available to the Covered Entity (or individual as directed) for access and amendment, and incorporate amendments as required.
6. **Accounting:** Provide an accounting of disclosures as required by HIPAA.
7. **Return/destruction:** Upon termination, return or destroy PHI as agreed (or retain if destruction is not feasible).

---

## Term and termination

- **Term:** [Start date] through [End date], or until terminated.
- **Termination:** Either party may terminate for material breach upon written notice and opportunity to cure (e.g. 30 days).
